Introduction

“I write to remember. I write to resist. I write to reclaim what history has tried to erase.”

Welcome! I’m Madison "Maddie P." Petaway, a poet, performer, and workshop host from Houston, Texas. My writing blends the deeply personal with history, focusing on memory, community, and resistance.

Biography

In 2020, I became Houston’s first Black female Youth Poet Laureate. My poem “To Be a Black Girl” has been featured in The New York Times and StudySync. I've performed at protests, literary events, and institutions across Texas, including TEDxUTAustin and Voters of Tomorrow Texas’ banned books protest in Austin.

Literary Work & Publications

  • Published Works: “To Be a Black Girl” (The New York Times)

Creative Philosophy

My poetry serves as a bridge between past and present, highlighting how history and memory shape identity. Inspired by poets like Richard Siken, my work ensures each poem is not just a recollection, but a demand for remembrance.

Awards & Fellowships

  • TEDxUTAustin Speaker & Winner (2022)
  • Texas Revue First Prize Winner (2022)
  • First Black Female Youth Poet Laureate of Houston (2020-2021)

Literary Community & Performance

As a former three-time member of Meta-Four Houston, I competed in poetry slams, learning firsthand the power of spoken word. I frequently perform at open mics and believe that to truly understand a literary scene, you must engage with both its largest stages and its underground spaces.
